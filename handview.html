HAND EMBROIDERY

Hand embroidery is a decorative art of stitching patterns or designs onto fabric using a needle and thread or yarn. This ancient craft has been practiced for centuries across different cultures and regions, and it remains popular for both functional and artistic purposes.

Embroidery dates back to the Neolithic period, with early examples found in ancient China, Egypt, and Greece. It evolved from a practical skill into an art form that was used to decorate garments, household items, and ceremonial objects.

Hand embroidery involves various types of stitches and techniques that can be used to create a range of effects.
